Perceived Importance of Government Funding for Research on Depression in EU Countries, 2020



How Europeans View the Importance of Funding for Depression Research

The data reveals a powerful message: the vast majority of the population believes that supporting mental health research is not only necessary but crucial.

In countries such as Croatia and Cyprus, over 70% of respondents consider funding for depression research to be extremely important, highlighting an urgent public demand for action. This trend is not isolated; across most of Europe, there is a clear priority placed on mental health, with Austria, Belgium, and Finland also showing high support in this category.

For some EU nations, a significant portion of the population believes in the importance of funding, but with slightly less urgency. This level of support still underscores the perceived value of mental health research.

The data paints a clear picture: European citizens are calling for government investment in depression research, with the majority viewing it as critically important. The urgency of these opinions serves as a reminder to policymakers that mental health support is a fundamental aspect of public health that deserves immediate attention.
